.about

obsessed with how things work — and how people find their way.

I've always been obsessed with how things work — and how people find their way. As a kid, I dismantled toys to uncover their hidden mechanics. In university, I studied geodetic surveying, fascinated by how maps quietly guide us through the world.

My path to design clicked after years on the frontlines of customer service. I loved helping people, but I realized I wanted to fix the roads, not just direct the traffic. UI/UX was the perfect intersection: digital spaces are simply modern maps, and every interaction is a signpost.

Today, instead of toys, I dismantle workflows. I believe the best designs don't rely on flashy technology; they rely on empathy and simplicity. When I'm not designing, I'm usually looking under the hood of emerging tech to see what makes it tick, or scribbling app ideas — most are terrible, until one isn't.

I'm drawn to the messy, human side of technology. Whether I'm mapping out a user's journey or geeking out over a slick interaction, I'm here to solve puzzles that make life a little kinder, and the journey a little smoother.
